Privacy Policy

How Omar Huertas LLC collects, uses, shares, and protects information across our website, our consulting and implementation services, and the applications we develop and publish.

Last updated: June 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Omar Huertas LLC (“Omar Huertas LLC,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information. It applies to: (a) our website at dbaomarhuertasllc.com; (b) our consulting, implementation, build, and advisory services; and (c) the mobile, desktop, and web applications we design, develop, and publish (collectively, the “Services”). By using the Services, you agree to this Policy.

1. Our roles (controller vs. processor)

For our website and our own business contacts, we act as a data controller. When we deliver services or build and operate systems on behalf of a client, we typically act as a data processor (or service provider), handling information only on that client’s documented instructions and under the applicable engagement agreement or Data Processing Addendum (DPA). In those cases, the client is the controller and their privacy policy governs.

2. Information we collect

a. Website visitors & prospects. Information you choose to submit — name, email, company, phone number, and the contents of your message. Our hosting and form providers may log standard technical data (IP address, browser type, pages requested) for security and reliability.

b. Clients. Business contact details, project information, and records of our work and correspondence.

c. Information processed for clients. In the course of consulting and implementation, we may access systems (e.g., ServiceNow, CMDB, ITSM, asset, and integration data) that contain personal or confidential data belonging to a client and their users. We process this strictly to perform the engagement, under confidentiality and the client’s instructions.

d. Our applications. Depending on the specific app, we may collect account identifiers (such as Sign in with Apple), content you create, device and diagnostic data, and usage analytics. Each app’s store listing (App Store / Google Play) and in-app disclosures describe exactly what that app collects. We do not knowingly collect more than is needed for the app to function.

3. How we use information

To respond to inquiries; scope, deliver, and support our services and applications; operate, secure, maintain, and improve the Services; process transactions and licenses; communicate with you; meet legal, tax, and contractual obligations; and protect against fraud, abuse, and security incidents. We do not use your information for automated decision-making that produces legal effects, and we do not sell your personal information.

4. Legal bases (GDPR/UK GDPR)

Where applicable, we rely on: consent (e.g., optional communications), contract (to provide services/apps you request), legitimate interests (to run and secure our business), and legal obligation. Where we act as a processor, the client establishes the legal basis.

5. How we share information

We do not sell or rent personal information. We share it only: (a) with vetted service providers / sub-processors (e.g., hosting, email, form delivery, analytics, app platforms, payment processors) bound by confidentiality and data-protection terms; (b) with a client, when we process data on their behalf; (c) to comply with law, legal process, or lawful requests; (d) to protect the rights, property, or safety of Omar Huertas LLC, our clients, or others; or (e) in connection with a business transfer (merger, acquisition, or asset sale), subject to this Policy.

6. Our applications — additional terms

  • Disclosures. Each app’s App Store/Google Play privacy label and in-app notices state the data it collects and why.
  • Permissions. Apps request device permissions (e.g., notifications, camera, files) only when needed for a feature, and you can manage them in your device settings.
  • Third-party SDKs. Apps may use platform and analytics SDKs (e.g., Apple, Google) subject to their terms.
  • Purchases. Any in-app purchases or subscriptions are processed by the app store; we do not receive your full payment-card details.
  • Account & data deletion. Where an app supports accounts, you may request deletion of your account and associated data by emailing us.
  • No sale; no ad tracking unless explicitly disclosed in that app’s listing.

7. Cookies & analytics

Our website does not set advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. We may use privacy-respecting, aggregate analytics and essential form/security cookies. If we add additional analytics, we will update this Policy and honor applicable consent requirements.

8. Data retention

We keep personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described, to satisfy legal, tax, or contractual requirements, or to resolve disputes — after which it is deleted or anonymized. Data processed on behalf of a client is retained per that client’s instructions and our agreement.

9. Data security

We apply reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information (such as access controls, encryption in transit, and least-privilege practices). No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. International transfers

We are based in the United States and may process information in the U.S. and other countries. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) for cross-border transfers.

11. Children’s privacy

Our website and services are intended for businesses and adults and are not directed to children under 16. Unless an app is expressly designated and disclosed as suitable for children (in which case we comply with COPPA and applicable app-store child-safety requirements), we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

12. Your privacy rights

EEA/UK (GDPR): rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict or object to processing, and data portability, and to withdraw consent.

California (CCPA/CPRA): rights to know, access, correct, and delete personal information; to opt out of “sale” or “sharing” (we do neither); to limit use of sensitive personal information; and to non-discrimination for exercising your rights.

To exercise any right, email omar@dbaomarhuertasllc.com. We will verify and respond within the timeframes required by law. If we process your data on behalf of a client, we will refer your request to that client. You may unsubscribe from business emails anytime by replying “unsubscribe.”

13. Third-party links & services

The Services may link to or integrate third-party sites and platforms (e.g., ServiceNow, LinkedIn, app stores, scheduling or form tools). We are not responsible for their privacy practices; review their policies.

14. Data breach

If a security incident affecting personal information occurs, we will investigate and notify affected parties and authorities as required by applicable law and our client agreements.

15. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date reflects the latest version, and material changes will be posted on this page.

16. Governing law

This Policy is governed by the laws of the State of North Carolina, USA, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles, except where mandatory local data-protection law applies to you.
