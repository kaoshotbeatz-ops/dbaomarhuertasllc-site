Our Story

From the back of the room to building my own.

The honest version — the pain, the people, and the purpose behind Omar Huertas LLC.

I built my first computer at seven. My stepfather worked for Wang, Northern Telecom, and Oxford — technology was in our house before I understood how rare that was. We grew up on Long Island, Puerto Rican in a place that made sure we knew we didn’t belong. They mocked my name. They told me what I’d never be.

The world wasn’t gentle. I’ve been spit on, jumped, stabbed, wrongly accused — told I’d never be more than a garbage man. There was a lot of pain before any success. But giving up was never a luxury I had, so I kept getting back up.

At 31, I went back to school and earned my CCNA. Brian didn’t see where I came from — he saw the work, and he took me for real. That was the first real yes. Mike Lombardo, the Mayor of ServiceNow, showed me the standard. I never forgot either of them.

Along the way Benker threw down a challenge — that a consultant had more certifications than my CSA. So in three weeks I earned five more to get to par. Not to win an argument — to prove the standard was never in doubt. That challenge made me sharper, and I’m grateful for it.

I earned every certification because the world demanded proof — never because the work needed it. They never changed how I treat the people who trust me. I give my whole heart now — the same heart I poured into billion-dollar platforms and into the families and futures behind the work.

The price was real. A high-school dropout with a GED, four kids before I ever had a career — I carried 24 credits a semester because I couldn’t afford two, doubling up, coding and studying through the nights. There were nights my wife and kids asked where Dad was. He was at the desk, building the future they deserved. The struggle is what got me here — not the safe nights. I’ll never hide that.

Raising four kids on $20k a year was its own kind of miracle. And everything that tried to take me out — the rollovers I shouldn’t have walked away from, the stabbings I shouldn’t have survived — didn’t. I’m still here. Something had a bigger plan for me, and I don’t waste it.

It came full circle. I met Mike in Orlando at Knowledge — the Mayor — but it was Benker who really brought me into his world. In 2023, when the Apex contract-to-hire at DD didn’t pan out, Benker reached out to bring me back to GlideFast. I was unemployed with four kids — real talk, it was terrifying. But the company Mike built still cared: they kept me on an extra three weeks when they didn’t have to. I cried. Onboarding was surreal — I’d had access ten years before I ever joined, like the seat was always mine and I just got the invite late. So I paid it forward — I got Brian into GlideFast, an Elite partner, where he still is today.

Now I’ve started my own — just registered, just beginning. Not for the biggest logo in the room. For honest work, done right, for the people the world overlooks. Nothing more. Nothing less.

Omar HuertasFounder & ServiceNow Architect
Why we exist

More than a company. A movement.

I was meant for this. Everything that tried to take me out failed — because there was purpose in it.

Omar Huertas LLC isn’t just consulting. It’s a movement — to help the helpless, mentor the overlooked, and give hope to the ones who stopped believing they had it, because the world made them doubt their own truth and talent.

I started this because I’ve watched it up close: a friend who outworks everyone, more than skilled, with a visa sponsorship held over his head like a leash — denied the break he earned. Talent should never be hostage to status, a name, or where you came from.

We back the deserving. We give the shot. On skill — nothing more, nothing less.
